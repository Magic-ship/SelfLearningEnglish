5 Base Rules of English Writing

  • 1. Try to avoid overly long sentences

    Long sentences aren't necessarily bad, but lose your audience, they are.

    As a rule of thumb, a sentence should not have more than 10 to 15 words.

  • 2. Each sentence should make a clear statement

    If should add to the statement that went before, a good paragraph is a serious of clear, linked statement.

    As a rule of thumb, a paragraph should not consist of 100 to 200 words.

      Change paragraphs when ...
    • A new character comes along
    • A new event happens
    • A new idea is introduced
    • The setting changes.
    • A new person is speaking
    • Time moves forward or backward a lot.
    • The "camera" moves
  • 3. Do not use words whose meanings you not sure of.
  • Try to avoid wanting to sound intelligent by using words that you don't understand. Know the word that you use.
  • If you need to use special words, look them up in dictionary before, or use words that you understand instead.
  • 4. Use concrete rather than vague language.

    Vague : The weather was of an extreme nature of the West Coast.

      This sentence raises frustrating questions:
    • When did this extreme weather occur?
    • What does of an "extreme weather" mean?
    • Where on the West Coast did this take place?

    Concrete : Califonia had unusually cold weather last week.

    Reminder : Try to avoid jargon. Use words that are appropriate for the reader.

  • 5. Don't use negatives to make a positive without good reason.

    Terrible : I don't know nothing about computers.

    Better: I don't know anything about computers.

    Better: I know nothing about computers.

    Unnecessary: He is not unwilling to help.

    Better: He is willing to help.

    Sometimes a not un-construction may be desirable, perhaps even necessary.

    Ex : The book is uneven but not uninteresting.
